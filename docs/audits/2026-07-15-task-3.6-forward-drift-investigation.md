## Executive conclusion

The frozen 71-line post-cutoff population is not evidence that the POS MAC
write formula drifted. All 71 stored costs are reproduced by the BTP recipe
state that was visible when the order was written:

| Mechanism | Lines | Current replay delta |
|---|---:|---:|
| BTP recipe version/effective-visibility change | 71 | -67,221 VND |
| Late PO receipt, optional newer population | 42 | +6,809 VND |
| Total investigated | 113 | -60,412 VND |

For 64 of the 71 frozen lines, selecting the BTP recipe effective at sale time
reproduces `cost_at_sale` exactly. For the other seven, the immediately
previous BTP recipe reproduces `cost_at_sale` exactly even though the recipe's
effective timestamp says the replacement was already active. The schema has no
independent recipe `recorded_at`, so those seven distinguish neither a
backdated recipe insert nor a stale application view; both are recipe
visibility mechanisms, not a MAC formula difference.

The audit replay is temporally asymmetric. The order line freezes its top-level
product/modifier recipe, but BTP shortfall decomposition is rebuilt from the
currently selected nested BTP recipe. A later BTP recipe version therefore
changes historical replay without changing stored COGS.

No production row was changed. No line is recommended for recompute from this
investigation.

## Write-time and replay path

Both paths use `buildLineConsumptionRows`. When direct BTP stock is short, the
shared allocator decomposes the shortfall into base ingredients:

```text
base ingredient quantity = BTP shortfall quantity
                         x BTP recipe ingredient quantity
                         / BTP batch yield
```

The POS then applies `computeMacCostFromUnitCosts` to the compact MAC state
returned for the sale timestamp. The audit applies
`computeMacCostForConsumptionRows` to the ledger as of the same timestamp.
The investigation ran both formulas on identical ledger, balance, recipe, and
consumption inputs. They differed on 0/113 lines.

The temporal mismatch occurs one level earlier:

1. At checkout, POS loads the BTP recipe state then visible and writes
   `cost_at_sale`.
2. The line snapshot preserves the product variant and modifier recipes, but
   not the nested BTP recipe version or batch yield used for shortfall.
3. The audit creates one current `buildSemiProductRecipeMaps` context and uses
   it for historical orders.
4. Changing a BTP recipe changes historical shortfall decomposition and thus
   the replayed MAC cost.

This is an audit replay/time-context gap and a snapshot-schema gap. The POS MAC
write formula itself was reproduced and is not implicated.

BTP-009 explains the other 39 frozen lines and -25,311 VND. Its change from
`RC-022` to `RC-030`, effective 2026-07-11 17:00 UTC, reduced `ING-020` from
100 to 50 while leaving the other recipe quantities unchanged.

For 64 lines sold before their respective cutovers, the historical effective
recipe exactly reproduces stored cost. Seven lines retain the old-recipe cost
after the declared cutover: four BTP-009 lines on July 12 and three BTP-002
lines on July 14. Replaying the immediately previous recipe exactly reproduces
all seven. Because recipe rows overload `created_at` as effective time and do
not retain an independent insertion timestamp, the evidence supports a recipe
visibility gap but cannot separate late insertion from stale cache.

## The 42 newer IDs

- Visibility lags are well above five minutes; none occupy the trigger's
  one-to-five-minute threshold gap.

These 42 belong to the existing Task 3.2 durable backdated-ledger review path.
They are not automatic recovery candidates.

## Recommendations

1. Do not recompute any of the frozen 71 lines. Their stored costs match the
   reconstructable write-time recipe state.
2. Feed Task 3.5 an explicit requirement to select nested BTP recipe versions
   as of each sale, rather than reuse one current recipe map for all orders.
3. For durable auditability, snapshot the nested BTP recipe version and batch
   yield used by shortfall decomposition, or persist equivalent version IDs on
   the order line/consumption evidence.
4. Separate recipe `effective_at` from immutable `recorded_at`. The current
   overloaded timestamp makes seven lines' visibility history unfalsifiable.
5. Route the 42 newer late-PO lines through Task 3.2; their durable detection
   events already exist.
6. Wait for Claude review before opening a forward-drift remediation task. No
   engine or audit fix is included here.
