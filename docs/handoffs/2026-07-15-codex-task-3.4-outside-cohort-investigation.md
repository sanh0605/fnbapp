## Goal

**Read-only investigation.** Classify the 224 outside-cohort mismatches into causal buckets so we can decide:

1. Which lines are safe to recompute under an existing causal theory (e.g., purchase-cost recovery, backdated ledger).
2. Which lines are new live drift (post-cutoff group) and require forward-looking detection, not historical recovery.
3. Which lines are pre-baseline-window (2026-04-20 to early June) and whether they represent a baseline-scope gap.
4. Whether the baseline audit script itself missed these lines (Task 3.5 dependency).

**No DB writes. No recovery apply. No migration. No MAC engine changes.**

## Hypotheses to test

For each: state whether evidence supports/rejects, with specific data.

### H1: Post-cutoff lines are new live backdating

- 71 lines dated after 2026-07-02 may be orders affected by PO/STOCK_ADJUST/PRODUCTION_YIELD rows backdated after the baseline was frozen.
- **Test**: For each of the 71 post-cutoff lines, query `backdated_ledger_events` (migration 0014) for visibility events affecting the consumed items, with `effective_at` between sale time and now. Cross-reference `Stock_Ledger.created_at > sale_date` and `effective_at <= sale_date`.

### H2: Post-cutoff lines are new engine drift

- MAC engine may have changed since the baseline freeze. Recent commits to `lib/mac-cogs.ts`, `lib/inventory-consumption.ts`, or `app/pos/actions.ts` could have shifted write-time MAC.
- **Test**: `git log --oneline --since=2026-07-02 -- lib/mac-cogs.ts lib/inventory-consumption.ts app/pos/actions.ts app/admin/orders/actions.ts`. For each commit, check whether the 71 post-cutoff lines were sold before or after the commit. If after, the commit may be causal.

### H3: Pre-cutoff outside-cohort lines are baseline scope gap

- 153 lines dated on/before 2026-07-02 are in the same time window as the baseline but were not in the 170-line list. They may have:
  - Appeared after the baseline was frozen (new writes to old orders via edit).
  - Been excluded by the original baseline script's filter (e.g., status filter, item filter).
  - Been in the original 170 but later edited/removed.
- **Test**: Compare the 153 pre-cutoff outside-cohort line IDs against the original baseline JSON. Confirm 0 overlap (otherwise the audit is buggy). Then inspect the original baseline script's selection criteria vs the live audit script's criteria. Flag any divergence.

### H4: Pre-baseline-window lines (2026-04-20 to early June)

- Earliest outside-cohort mismatch is 2026-04-20. The baseline was scoped to a specific window; lines outside that window were never investigated.
- **Test**: Count lines by UTC sale date. If the pre-June population is material (say >20 lines), classify as a separate `PRE_BASELINE_WINDOW` bucket and recommend either extending the baseline or accepting as historical drift.

### H5: Sign asymmetry between locked and outside cohorts

- Locked cohort is net +120,716 VND (over-stored). Outside cohort is net ~-262,013 VND (under-stored). Different signs suggest different mechanisms.
- **Test**: Within the outside cohort, split by sign. Are the negative-delta lines concentrated in specific products, BTPs, or date ranges? Or distributed evenly? A concentration may point to one dominant cause.

### H6: BTP shortfall recurrence

- Task 3.3 found BTP_SHORTFALL was the dominant pattern (132/170 lines). The outside cohort may show the same pattern if the shortfall mechanism is still active.
- **Test**: For each outside-cohort line, parse `recipe_snapshot_json` and consumption rows. Flag lines where consumed quantity exceeds available BTP stock at sale time. Compare rate vs the locked baseline.

### H7: Edit-order side effects

- Editing old orders via `editOrderV2` writes new consumption rows with current MAC, but old `cost_at_sale` may not be re-pinned. This could create new mismatches on old lines.
- **Test**: For each outside-cohort line, query `Order_Events` for `EDIT` events on the parent order after sale date. Flag lines where edit occurred and `cost_at_sale` was not updated.
